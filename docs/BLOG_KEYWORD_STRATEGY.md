# Barfliz Blog Keyword Strategy

**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Niche:** Social drinking app (bar discovery, real-time crowd, social meet-ups)
**Source data:** `barfliz_keyword_research.json` (Google Trends 90d US, Reddit hot/top posts, Perplexity)

---

## TL;DR

Five pillar topics, each with a cluster of long-tail keywords, drive 80% of the blog SEO opportunity. Three of the five (happy hour, drink specials, drinking games) have rising queries hitting 4,000% to 92,000% growth, which means the SERPs are still soft and rankable.

Priority pillars by volume × intent fit:

| Rank | Pillar | Why it wins |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Happy hour finder** | Highest evergreen volume (interest 66/100). Direct app intent. Geo + day-of-week long tails are wide open. |
| 2 | **Drinking games** | Interest 43/100. Massive rising queries on 2-person, card, party variants. Underserved SERP. |
| 3 | **Non-alcoholic / mindful drinking** | Interest 36/100. Growing trend (sober curious, dry-ish, mocktails). Almost no app-content overlap. |
| 4 | **Best bars near me / where to drink tonight** | Interest 34 to 64/100. Pure Barfliz intent. Hard SEO (Yelp/Google dominate) but local + niche angles winnable. |
| 5 | **Bar etiquette + ordering + tipping** | Reddit + Quora goldmine. Low competition. Brand-builder content. |

## Recommended Cadence

| Frequency | Content type | Effort |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Weekly (1) | Pillar / cluster post | 1,500 to 2,500 words |
| Weekly (1) | City-specific guide | 1,000 to 1,500 words (rotate top 25 US cities) |
| Bi-weekly (1) | Seasonal / event post | Pre-written + scheduled |
| Monthly (1) | Big visual / data post | 3,000+ words, social-shareable |

Total: ~10 posts/month. At ~150 words/min with Claude drafting + Steve editing, ~6 hours/month total time.

---

## What to Skip (Low ROI)

- Generic cocktail recipes (r/cocktails is saturated, low purchase intent)
- Bartending career content (wrong audience)
- Pure alcohol education (Wikipedia + corporate sites dominate)
- "What is happy hour" type content (search intent is finder, not definition)
